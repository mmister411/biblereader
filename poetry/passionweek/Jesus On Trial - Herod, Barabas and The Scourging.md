# Jesus On Trial - Herod, Barabas and The Scourging

While no fault was detected by Pilate at first,
The Chief Priest and the elders insist He's the worst.  
Bewildered at Jesus's slack to react
While realizing the pharisees lacking of facts.
Pilate prods once again "Don't you hear their harsh claims?"
But his silence again renders Pilate amazed.
In the clamor they mention he's "Galilean". 
To which Pilate recalls is in Herod's region.
 
On to Herod he passes this curious case. 
While Herod had wanted to see what await.
Herod hoped he could see any miracle worked. 
So he questioned him, pressing him, probing for quirks.
Not a spark or a word or a twitch of a lip.
Unamused by this, Herod then tightened his grip.

The chief priests were there also to strongly protest.
With his Guards Herod mocked at his distinguished guest.
Herod had him adorned in a dyed purple robe
As a message to Pilate that He was a joke.
Beforehand, these two rulers were not on the mend
But surprisingly, now, over this they made friends.  

By now much of Jerusalem gathered around.
Their murmurs converge to an uneasy sound.
Pilate gestures to summon the rulers and priests 
and the people to figure out whom they’d release.

“You’ve brought me this man whom you understand 
went and wrought on the people such dastardly plans.  
I have lifted my hand so to search and to seek 
out the truth. As it stands now your case here is weak.
I discovered his clan hails from Herod district.
When to Herod I sent him to find any dent, 
Neither Herod detected real legal dissent.
I find nothing condemning this man here to death
Zero reason that merits his ceasing of breath.
For this reason I’ll scourge him and queue for release 
It’s on you all to figure out what equals peace.”
 
On the feast day a norm was return of a captive.
Pilot pressed to choose Jesus but their ears were not active.
“Free Barabas!!” they roared.  The chief priests were proactive 
 With goading  the crowd toward who they should ask for.

Now Barabas was one for the stirring of dramas, 
Like rebellion and murder, true criminal saga. 
Pilate’s wife intervened, sending Pilate a message
Regarding this “Jesus”.  He stalls to address it. 
“Leave that man there alone, have nothing to do
With that man from my dream that has brought me the blues.”
The unraveling crowd with no respite for silence 
Now devolved to a mob was a problem for Pilate.

Pilate calls out again, “What then shall I do
With this Jesus, called Christ, also ‘King of the Jews’?”
With a roar came the answer, the crowd surging tight,
“Crucify him! We want him removed from our sight!”

“For what reason? What treason? What’s really the claim?”
But they shouted much louder, still  chanting the same.
“Crucify! Crucify!” Hanging thick in the air
Resonating the limestone walls in the square.

Seeing riot was near and no peace would reside
Pilate signaled for water to be brought to his side.
He washed both his hands as the crowd strained to see,
“I am clean of his blood; there’s no blame here for me.”

“All his blood be on us and our children as well!”
In near unison loudly their voices had swelled.
So Barabbas was freed and the case was now closed. 
They delivered up Jesus to reap what they sowed.

Pilate, feigning no choice, sent the man to be scourged
In the view of the public as mockeries surge.
Set up in advance was a pillar or post
Specifically meant for this whipping with ropes.
Many ropes filled with spurs of metal or stone 
With a handle for dismantling skin, flesh and bone.
 
They progress the event by stripping him bare
Making sure all his skin is exposed to the air. 
They begin to attach his restraints to the pillar
With his rear facing out as the moment draws nearer
The flesh on his back was compressed by the fact 
That his wrist were affixed overhead on a tack.
The prepares for the hits the ensure bring the max
Effectiveness packed in each set of attacks.

SHWISH!! FWACK!! And.. SHWISH!! FWACK!! As they alternate strikes.
The hooks can get stuck so they tug at the spikes
SHWISH!! FWACK!! And.. SHWISH!! FWACK!! Blood oozes to the Ground.
Visceral echoes ring out with hide-rending sounds.
A mind bending sight; snapping veins, showing bone.
One-hundred percent of men scourged don’t last long.


Next The soldiers took Jesus to move him away 
from the square to the hall where the garrison stayed.
Since they stripped Jesus  clothing they  place on a robe
Of scarlet or purple to mock and to probe.
They grab up some thorns to reshape as a crown
And the place on his head as the force it in down.
With a Reed placed in hand the old mocking ensues
The say “Hail, everyone, this is King of the Jews”
The spit in his face and strike with the Reed 
For public disgracing they sharply succeed.

Presenting again Pilate speaks to the horde
“Behold Jesus! I find no evil accord.”
But no change in the Priest or the crowds old refrain
“CRUCIFY HIM!” The chanted demand still remains.

You’d have me crucify this your king” Pilate reams, 
“We’ve no king but of Caesar” the priests nearly sing.
Pilate figures no winning or changing their minds
So he folds. Crucifixion begins to unwind.
