# Gethsemane - Garden of Prayer

When Jesus was finished with speaking His words, 
The hour was nearing, a time was reserved.
An appointment to meet and a cup there to drink, 
So Jesus got going with no time to blink. 
The breezes were cooling. The sun fled away.
Under moonlight they traveled with little to say. 

The shadows of olive trees shaded the ground
of the slopes of the valley.  The trickling sound 
Of the stream just ahead brought a peace to the air.  
Anticipation faithfully threatened despair.
When they’d reach their next stop over, what would they find?
Prior warnings of destiny swirl in their minds. 

They went onward crossing the river named Kidron, 
and into a garden, in dark after even, 
named Gethsemane, with disciples in tow. 
Though He told them what's coming, they thought it not so.  

“Strike the shepherd,” He said, “and the sheep will go scatter,”
A line from the prophets to transpire after.
But Peter replied with devotion and flare,
"Though all others may fall I will always be there,
Even if I must die I will never reject."

Then Jesus dissected His zealous projection
"The rooster will crow as you often have seen,
You'll deny me three times before the rooster crows three"
Still Peter insisted, “No! Never with me!”
And the others joined in with that same loyalty.

Then the timing had come, and his purpose was due. 
This same hour, aforetime, ordained to come true. 
He became sore amazed, almost dazed from the weight. 
All the sins of the world had begun to translate
Onto him.  All the wrath began taking it's toll. 
Then he said "Unto death is my sorrowful soul" 
And to "terry ye, watch with me, this, is your role." 

Then He stepped a few steps and he fell on His face. 
Often prayer was His target and such was the case. 
On the ground and astounded, beginning to pray:
"Oh Abba, my Father, please hear me this day: 
"All impossible things are still things unto you. 
Everything that's a thing is a thing you can do."
If this cup that I have can go passing me by 
then remove it far from me as fast as can fly. 
But if I must drink it then that shall come true. 
Not my will but thy will, Father will as You do. 

He then went from His praying to where Peter was laying,
and found him asleep, so then Jesus went saying,
“Simon? You’re asleep? Could you not watch for one hour? 
Watch and pray that you don't fall at temptation's power.
While the Spirit is ready to do what I speak; 
For the Spirit is strong but the flesh, it is weak. “

Then again, He withdrew, to a stone's throw away.
Where He kneeled to the ground as He started to pray.
"O my Father" He said, "If it be in Your will, 
Let this cup pass from me, and another fulfill.  
But nevertheless, if this ending must come, 
Then I drink.  Not my will but thy will shall be done. “
Then an angel appeared so to strengthen the Son.  

And returning again, His disciples were sleep
For their eyes were quite heavy, and flesh was quite weak.  
Then awaking to Him they knew not what to say 
Then to them said our LORD,” why just sleep time away?
Why just sleep? Why just snore? Now return to your station
Arise, so to pray, lest ye enter temptation”. 

He departs once again for a third time to pray. 
He repeats the same words with more earnestness laid. 
Then came agony, travesty, angst and great gloom. 
Till He sweat out His blood as our sins became wounds.
While the wrath crushed His soul, to make us wrath-immune. 
By His stripes we are healed. For our lives, blood is spilled. 
So to justify many as Father has willed,
And for love of your soul, all of this is fulfilled. 

He arose from His prayers with a third round complete.
His disciples were sleep from the sorrow and grief. 
They were needing relief while their eyelids hung low. 
But this time He said "Sleep, take your rest, soon I go. 
It is time, it has come, Son of Man is betrayed
to the hands of the sinners that He came to save.
