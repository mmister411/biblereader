# Jesus On Trial - Pilate & Judas

As the dawn became day on Jerusalem's streets
And the sprawl of sun rays showed the night was complete.
The Sanhedrin convened to decide what was left 
for completing their plot to put Jesus to death.
Once they bound him they left from the High priest's estate
To the Governor's building to settle his fate.  
Pontius Pilate was filling this role at that time.
To this Roman they need to prove Jesus's crimes.
To be clean for the Feast they refused to go in
If they did then the Passover they'd not attend. 

So they beckoned for Pilate to meet them outside 
And receive the accused they presumed to provide.
Having stepped out to hear their assignment of blame 
He asks plainly: "What offense of this man do you claim?" 
They deflected, "If guilt were not plainly the case, 
we would never have brought Him to stand in this place." 
"Judge this man by yourselves. You have laws of your own" 
His frustration was audibly set in his tone. 
"We can't have any man put to death without Rome" 
Which fulfills by what death Jesus makes his way home. 
 
Then Judas Iscariot saw what took place. 
He was moved to despair with remorse on his face.   
His imprudence imparted an ending quite grim. 
There’s an innocent man that his hands helped condemn.   
He resolved to return every piece he had earned 
Since his conscience now squirmed for the friend he had burned.

The sounds were distinct of the shuffles and clinks
Which he knew all to well, every coin with its plink.
Then a WHUFF cuts the air from the sound of the purse
That he chucked with a grunt catapulted by hurt.
Then a thud on the dust covered paving stone street.
People pause as they’re hearing coins roll and go plink.
“I have sinned in betrayal of innocent blood.”
“Meaning what to us?” Silence rushed in like a flood.
“That’s for you to deal with”; Judas broken and stunned.

Judas walked a few paces. A line had been crossed.
It was too much to bear.  It seems all had been lost.
He acquired a rope and selected a tree.
He supposed that this way was the way it must be.
Whether rope or the branch, one had ripped and he dropped.
And his corpse was torn through as it smashed on a rock. 

The money exchanged to put Jesus to death,
Thirty pieces of silver remained unaddressed.
‘The hire of blood is unfit for the temple.”
Said the priest as if this was a case that was simple.
So they purchased a field with the spurious funds 
where they bury their foreigners; Not their own sons.
It was first “Potter’s Field” then redubbed “Field of Blood.”
For the funds and possibly where Judas went thud.
Now, more words of the Lord by the prophets come true. 
“And they weighed out my wage: thirty pieces of silver”
Carried out without pause, hesitation or filter.

We resume to the room of the stone judgment hall.
The chief priests are refusing to enter at all.  
Pontius Pilate proceeds from outside where it’s bright 
To the shadowy hall spliced with splinters of light.
The echoes of sandals flip flop in with tension
Settings guards on alert as they pop to attention.
Jesus notices Pilate while bruised but composed.
Pilate calls out to Jesus, confused but still bold. 

“Are you king of the Jews?” Incredulity flows.
Both contempt and wonderment fuse in his tone.
“Are you asking for you or because you were told?”
Unfazed, Jesus’ face doesn’t fumble or fold.
“Am I Jew?” Pilate quips. “You’re own chief priests are stunned
And Have brought you to me, so now what have you done?”
“My kingdom is not of this world or this life.
If it was then my servants would rise up and fight.
That I not be delivered to Jewish control
But my Kingdom is not yet. The clock has not tolled.”

“So you are a king then?” He impatiently probes.
Jesus answers him calmly: “You say I’m a king.
For this cause I was born and I entered the scene:
To bear witness to truth.  It is clear to the point 
For those of the truth Because they hear my voice.”
“What is truth?” Pilate glibly remarks in annoyance.
